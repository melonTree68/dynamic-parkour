As we approach the end of the semester, please prepare and submit the final report for your course project.

## Submission Requirements

### Format

The final paper must be written using the [RSS conference LaTeX template](https://roboticsconference.org/docs/paper-template-latex.tar.gz).

The paper should be organized as a complete technical report and include the following components:

- Abstract
- Introduction
- Related Work
- Methodology / System Design
- Experiments and Evaluation
- Results and Discussion
- Conclusion
- References

### Page Limit

- Maximum length: **8 pages**
- References are excluded from the page limit.
- Papers exceeding the page limit may receive a penalty.

### Content Expectations

Compared with the proposal and midterm report, the final paper should present the complete project, including:

- Clear problem formulation and motivation
- Technical approach and implementation details
- Experimental setup and evaluation methodology
- Quantitative and/or qualitative results
- Analysis of successes, limitations, and future improvements

Projects are expected to demonstrate substantial progress beyond the proposal and midterm stages.

## Submission Deadline

**Deadline:** End of Week 18 (July 5, 2026.)

Late submissions may be subject to penalties according to the course policy.

## Submission Materials

Please submit:

1. **Final paper (PDF format).**
2. **Code projects organized using git, as announced in the previous email.**
3. Supplementary materials (optional)
   - Presentation slides.
   - Videos or demonstrations.
   - Project webpages links.
   - Other resources that describe your efforts on the project.

## Evaluation Criteria

The final project evaluation will consider:

- Technical novelty and creativity
- Soundness of methodology
- Quality of implementation
- Experimental evaluation
- Clarity of writing and presentation
- Overall project completion

## Academic Integrity

All submitted work must be original. Proper citation must be provided for all referenced papers, codebases, datasets, and external resources. **Academic misconduct or plagiarism will result in disciplinary action and may lead to failure of the project.**

We look forward to seeing your final results and project demonstrations.
